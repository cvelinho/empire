Things to do

CONCERT AT ALICE TULLY HALL

World famous for Chamber Music and home to the New York Film Festival

Since it opened in 1969, Alice Tully Hall has been home to the New York Film Festival and the Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center. In addition to world-class chamber music, Alice Tully Hall hosts numerous virtuoso concerts on its cathedral-sized, Swiss-made pipe organ.
The hall now has a three-story glass lobby which features a café and bar, and a cantilevered extension juts out over a sunken plaza at the corner of Broadway and 65th Street, now a meeting place for Lincoln Center visitors and the general public.
http://www.lincolncenter.org/venue/alice-tully-hall

NIGHT AT THE METROPOLITAN OPERA

Enjoy 2016 Season favorites; Aida, La Boheme, Barber of Seville

Acoustically amongst the most famous and well known in the world under the musical direction of James Levine. Everyone should experience an Opera at The Met just once. Take a picture in front of the Revson Fountain which has performed cameo roles in many blockbuster films including The Producers, Annie Hall, Moonstruck and Ghostbusters (the original).
http://www.lincolncenter.org/venue/metropolitan-opera-house

SHOPPING AT TIME WARNER CENTER

The Shops at Columbus Circle; Armani Exchange, Stuart Weitzman, Thomas Pink, Tourneau et al.

Prime Shopping in the heart of Manhattan in the Time Warner Center across the street from the resplendent Central Park.
http://www.theshopsatcolumbuscircle.com

GO TO CARNEGIE HALL

Experience where greatness has performed at Carnegie Hall

The hallowed performance stage at Carnegie Hall has been graced over its storied life by the greatest of greats from the Big Band and Jazz era featuring Count Basie and Billie Holiday to rock legends such as The Beatles and Led Zeppelin along with world famous lectures given by the likes of Mark Twain no less.
http://www.carnegiehall.org

PICNIC IN CENTRAL PARK

Enjoy a sunny day in the beautifully verdant Central Park New York

Grab a picnic basket from Whole Foods Market in the Time Warner Center and head over to Sheep Meadow to lounge in the grass or over to Strawberry Fields where someone is always playing a tribute to John Lennon.
http://www.centralpark.com
